Privacy

Private by default

Waymark is a personal field journal. The web app stores notes in the browser on your device. The iOS app stores native notes on your device. Waymark does not publish your notes or provide a community posting system.

Online questions

When you use Ask, the place, question, and selected lens are sent to Waymark's server so it can retrieve public reference sources and produce an answer. If the server is configured with an AI provider, that material may be sent to the provider for processing. Do not submit private names, exact real-time locations, future itineraries, affiliations, or sensitive personal information.

Microphone and speech

Microphone and speech-recognition access is requested only after you choose to dictate. Typed capture remains available. Waymark does not request device location, camera, contacts, or photo-library access in this release.

Data control

You can delete browser records using “Delete local data.” Native iOS notes can be deleted individually or all at once in Privacy. Removing the app may also remove locally stored app data.

Sources and accuracy

Place answers may use public sources such as Wikimedia projects and official websites. Generated explanations can be incomplete or inaccurate. Verify important facts and do not rely on Waymark for emergency, navigation, medical, legal, or safety guidance.

Contact

Privacy and support information is maintained on the Waymark support page. The App Store listing also provides the publisher's monitored contact channel.
