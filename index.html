U.S. road trip journal

A private map of the places, states, and road notes you want to remember.

Noted States is a private U.S. road trip journal and travel record map for people who want to remember what they noticed, not just where they went. Mark visited states, cities, national parks, campuses, stadiums, and road stops; capture voice or text field notes; then compare your personal journey with clean Factbook layers for population, climate, income, industries, demographics, and elections.

Use it for an America road trip, Amtrak ride, study-abroad memory map, farm stay journal, or a simple visited-states tracker. Your private notes stay in your browser unless you choose to create a share card or limited friend link.

FAQ

What makes Noted States different?

What is Noted States?

Noted States is a private AI field journal and U.S. road trip map. It helps you record visited states and meaningful places, save quick observations, and turn travel memories into a map you can actually understand later.

Is this a route tracker or travel planner?

No. Navigation apps remember the route. Noted States remembers the noticing: questions, conversations, food memories, farmstay reflections, and the places that changed how a state felt to you.

What is My Journal vs. Factbook?

My Journal is subjective: your notes, visited places, and emotional memory map. Factbook is objective: state-by-state layers such as population size, climate zones, industries, income, demographics, and 2024 presidential results.

Can I share my journey?

Yes. You can create a visual journey card or limited friend link. Raw private notes are not published automatically.
